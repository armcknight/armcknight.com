I recently saw someone bring up a curve I've seen before, and it reminded me of another one I'd been thinking about recently in the context of LLMs, which reminded me of a third one I saw a long time ago (and which I've searched for several times in the past without success, but Google must've improved since then, because it just now served it right up to me). I'll present them in that respective order below.

I think all three help to account for perceptual shifts in our own abilities and the utility of new technologies as we spend time using them. The "learning curve" in sum total, if you will.

# The Gartner Hype Cycle Curve

This curve models the popularity of new technologies and helps explain mismatches in expectations vs reality. Important to keep in mind that neither expectations nor reality are static. I think this one helps capture where they equilibrate.

{% include blog-post-image.html source="gartner-hype-cycle-curve.jpg" alt="The Gartner hype cycle curve shows how perception of a given tool's utility changes over time." %}

# The Technological S-Curve

Not only do expectations and reality shift around a specific tool's ability, but the total set of tools available also grows: new tools are made all the time. Getting comfortable with one can lead a person to build a new one on top of it, or orchestrate a collection of them. Then, bam, a new tool is born, another [brick](https://xkcd.com/2347) in the tower of babel.

{% include blog-post-image.html source="technology-s-curve.png" alt="The technological S-curve shows how innovation can create infinite layers of abstraction and utility as tools are improved and compined." %}

# The Artistic Ability Curve

Whoa now, we're taking lessons from the art world, presented by a guy that can't stand hearing people talk about how their code is poetry?

Yes, because at the end of the day they're both still creative pursuits, governed by subjectivity and afloat in a turbulent sea of preferences. See, I can do adjectives and metaphors, too!

This third curve helps explain the ebbs and flows in one's view of their own creative abilities, as they gain experience, refine their techniques and incorporate newer technologies into their repertoire.

{% include blog-post-image.html source="self-evaluation-curve.png" alt="The Self-Evaluation Curve shows how as our ability to evaluate our work improves, our actual ability appears to degrade, and the curves for evaluation and ability constantly inflect around each other." %}

# The Learning Curve

Can these curves be combined, summed, solved as a system of equations?

The parameters of each one will be different for each individual. We will all have different initial conditions when embarking upon a career path or picking up a new tool.

As I like to say: always be learnin'. The most important thing for me is that I keep climbing upwards. Don't let yourself think you're relegated to a single hype cycle, asymptotically plateauing. There's always higher ground to be reached!
